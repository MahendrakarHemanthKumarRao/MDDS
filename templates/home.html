Heart Disease

Heart disease is caused by factors like high blood pressure, cholesterol, smoking, poor diet, lack of exercise, and genetics. It accounts for 32% of global deaths, with higher rates in low- and middle-income countries.

Liver Disease

Liver disease is caused by factors like viral infections, excessive alcohol consumption, obesity, and genetic disorders. It affects approximately 1 in 10 people globally, accounting for about 2% of all deaths, with rising prevalence in low-income regions.

Kidney Disease

Kidney disease is caused by diabetes, high blood pressure, glomerulonephritis, and urinary tract issues. It affects about 850 million people globally, accounting for roughly 1.5 million deaths annually, with increasing prevalence in low- and middle-income countries.

Pneumonia Disease

Pneumonia is caused by infections from bacteria, viruses, or fungi, often following respiratory infections. It affects around 450 million people annually, accounting for about 2.5 million deaths, especially impacting young children and the elderly worldwide.

Breast Cancer Disease

Breast cancer is caused by genetic mutations, hormonal factors, age, and lifestyle choices. It affects about 2.3 million women annually, representing 11.7% of all cancer cases globally, making it the most common cancer among women.

Brain Tumor Disease

A brain tumor is an abnormal growth of cells in the brain, which can be benign or malignant. Causes include genetic mutations, radiation exposure, and family history. Over 8 million people die worldwide from brain tumors annually. Symptoms vary based on the tumor's size, location, and type. Treatment typically involves surgery, radiation, and chemotherapy. Early detection is crucial for better outcomes.
