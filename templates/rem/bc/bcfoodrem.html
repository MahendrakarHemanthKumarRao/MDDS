Fruits and Vegetables

  • Dark Leafy Greens - Kale, spinach, and arugula are rich in antioxidants and fiber, which can reduce inflammation.
  • Broccoli - Contains sulforaphane, a compound that may inhibit cancer cell growth.
  • Berries - Blueberries, strawberries, and raspberries are packed with antioxidants that support immune function.
  • Carrots - High in beta-carotene, which is believed to protect against certain types of cancer.
  • Tomatoes - Rich in lycopene, an antioxidant linked to reducing the risk of breast cancer.

Healthy Proteins

  • Fatty Fish - Salmon, mackerel, and sardines provide omega-3 fatty acids that may reduce inflammation.
  • Legumes - Lentils, chickpeas, and black beans are plant-based protein sources rich in fiber.
  • Eggs - A high-quality protein source with essential nutrients for recovery.
  • Tofu and Tempeh - Soy-based proteins that contain isoflavones, which may have protective effects.

Whole Grains

  • Quinoa - A complete protein with essential amino acids and high fiber content.
  • Brown Rice - A nutritious alternative to white rice with more fiber and vitamins.
  • Oats - Rich in soluble fiber, which supports digestive health and helps manage weight.

Healthy Fats

  • Avocados - Packed with healthy monounsaturated fats and vitamins.
  • Nuts and Seeds - Almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, and flaxseeds are great sources of healthy fats and nutrients.
  • Olive Oil - A heart-healthy fat that supports overall wellness.

Additional Nutrition Tips

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated and support body functions.
  • Limit processed foods and sugary drinks to reduce inflammation.
  • Incorporate a variety of colorful fruits and vegetables for a range of nutrients.
  • Consult with a dietitian for personalized dietary recommendations.
