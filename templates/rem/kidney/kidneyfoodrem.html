Low-Potassium Fruits and Vegetables

  • Red Bell Peppers - Low in potassium and rich in vitamins A, C, and B6. Contains lycopene, an antioxidant that promotes overall health.
  • Cauliflower - Low in potassium and packed with fiber, vitamin C, and folate. Helps in detoxifying the body.
  • Blueberries - High in antioxidants and low in potassium. Supports kidney health and protects against inflammation.
  • Garlic - Enhances flavor without sodium and contains anti-inflammatory properties that support kidney function.
  • Cabbage - Low in potassium and a good source of vitamin K, vitamin C, and fiber. Aids in digestion and kidney support.
  • Apples - Low in potassium and high in fiber, helping to regulate blood sugar and cholesterol levels.
  • Pineapple - Low in potassium and high in bromelain, which reduces inflammation and supports digestion.
  • Cranberries - Promote urinary tract health and protect against urinary infections. Low in potassium.
  • Onions - Low in potassium and rich in chromium, which helps in metabolizing fats and carbs, reducing kidney strain.

Protein Sources for Kidney Health

  • Egg Whites - High-quality protein with low phosphorus content. Ideal for maintaining muscle without overburdening the kidneys.
  • Fish - Choose low-mercury fish like salmon, cod, and tilapia. Bake, broil, roast, or steam to reduce sodium and phosphorus.
  • Nuts and Seeds - Good sources of protein and healthy fats without high phosphorus content.
  • Legumes - Peas, lentils, and beans are plant-based protein options with minimal phosphorus.

Low-Phosphorus and Sodium Fats

  • Olive Oil and Avocado Oil - Healthy fats that are low in sodium and phosphorus.
  • Soft Margarine - Choose with vegetable oil as the first ingredient.
  • Limit saturated fats from animal products.
  • Avoid trans fats found in processed and packaged snacks.

Additional Kidney-Friendly Tips

  • Consult a dietitian for personalized dietary guidance.
  • Monitor fluid intake, especially in conditions like dialysis.
  • Limit high-potassium foods like bananas, potatoes, and oranges.
  • Reduce sodium intake by using herbs, spices, and lemon juice.
