Low Sodium Diet

  • Avoid processed and packaged foods (e.g., chips, canned soups, and deli meats).
  • Use fresh herbs, lemon, or vinegar for seasoning instead of salt.

Controlled Protein Intake

  • Include moderate amounts of high-quality proteins such as:
    • Skinless poultry
    • Fish (e.g., salmon, trout)
    • Eggs (egg whites preferred)
  • Limit red meat and processed proteins.

Potassium Management

  • Avoid high-potassium foods such as bananas, oranges, potatoes, tomatoes, and spinach.
  • Choose low-potassium options like:
    • Apples, berries, grapes, and pineapple
    • Cauliflower, cabbage, and green beans

Phosphorus Control

  • Avoid foods rich in phosphorus such as dairy products, nuts, seeds, and colas.
  • Opt for phosphorus binders (as recommended by a doctor) and consume alternatives like almond milk.

Hydration

  • Monitor fluid intake as per medical advice to prevent overloading the kidneys.

Iron and Anemia Management

  • Include iron-rich foods:
    • Lean meats, fortified cereals, and egg yolks
  • Pair with Vitamin C sources (e.g., strawberries, bell peppers) to enhance absorption.

Foods to Avoid

  • Processed meats, salty snacks, high-sodium sauces (soy sauce, ketchup), and fast food.
  • Avoid high-protein fad diets like keto.

Additional Notes

  • Consult a registered dietitian for personalized meal plans tailored to the individual's lab results and health conditions.
  • For those with kidney disease, closely monitor specific nutrients (e.g., sodium, potassium, phosphorus, and protein) and consult with healthcare providers for appropriate portions.

Additional Kidney-Friendly Tips

  • Consult a dietitian for personalized dietary guidance.
  • Monitor fluid intake, especially in conditions like dialysis.
  • Limit high-potassium foods like bananas, potatoes, and oranges.
  • Reduce sodium intake by using herbs, spices, and lemon juice.
