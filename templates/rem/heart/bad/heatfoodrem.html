Whole Grains

  • Brown rice, quinoa, oatmeal, and whole-grain bread to provide fiber and improve heart health.

Leafy Green Vegetables

  • Spinach, kale, and broccoli rich in antioxidants and vitamins.

Fatty Fish

  • Salmon, mackerel, and sardines, which are high in omega-3 fatty acids to reduce inflammation and lower blood pressure.

Nuts and Seeds

  • Almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, and flaxseeds as sources of healthy fats and protein.

Legumes and Beans

  • Lentils, chickpeas, and black beans to add protein and fiber while maintaining low cholesterol levels.

Low-Fat Dairy

  • Yogurt and skim milk to ensure calcium intake without saturated fats.

Fruits

  • Berries, oranges, and apples, which are high in antioxidants and low in sugar.

Healthy Oils

  • Olive oil and avocado oil for cooking instead of saturated fats.

Herbs and Spices

  • Turmeric, garlic, ginger, and cinnamon to enhance flavor and provide health benefits.

Limit or Avoid

  • Processed foods, sugary beverages, red meat, fried foods, and excessive salt.

Additional Tips

  • Consult a dietitian for personalized advice
  • Balance calorie intake with daily energy expenditure
  • Limit high-calorie, low-nutrition foods
  • Season foods with herbs, spices, and lemon juice instead of salt
