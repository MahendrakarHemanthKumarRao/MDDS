Fruits and Vegetables

  • Eat 5 or more servings per day
  • Good sources of fiber, vitamins, and minerals
  • Low in fat, calories, sodium, and cholesterol
  • Eat whole fruits instead of drinking juice for more fiber

Grains

  • Choose whole-grain foods for at least half of daily grain intake
  • Sources: whole-wheat bread, brown rice, whole-grain cereals and pasta
  • Provide fiber, vitamins, minerals, and complex carbohydrates
  • Limit high-fat baked goods and refined grains

Protein Sources

  • Good sources: fish, poultry, seafood, peas, lentils, nuts, eggs
  • Fish recommendations:
    • Eat at least 2 servings of low-mercury fish per week
    • Cook by baking, broiling, roasting, steaming, boiling, or microwaving
  • Consider meatless meals and plant-based proteins
  • Dairy provides protein, calcium, and essential vitamins

Fats and Cholesterol

  • Avoid saturated and trans fats
  • Saturated fat sources:
    • Animal products: butter, cheese, whole milk, fatty meats
    • Coconut and palm oils
  • Avoid trans fats in:
    • Packaged snacks
    • Hydrogenated or partially-hydrogenated oils
    • Commercial baked goods
  • Choose soft margarine with vegetable oil as first ingredient

Additional Tips

  • Consult a dietitian for personalized advice
  • Balance calorie intake with daily energy expenditure
  • Limit high-calorie, low-nutrition foods
  • Season foods with herbs, spices, and lemon juice instead of salt
