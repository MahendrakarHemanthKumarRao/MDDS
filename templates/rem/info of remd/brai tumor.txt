If Brain Tumor Disease is Predicted:
Anti-inflammatory foods:

Turmeric (contains curcumin) – helps reduce inflammation.
Fatty fish (e.g., salmon, mackerel, sardines) – rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which help reduce inflammation and may promote brain health.
Berries (e.g., blueberries, strawberries) – rich in antioxidants, which can help protect brain cells from damage.
High-fiber foods:

Whole grains (e.g., quinoa, brown rice, oats) – fiber helps in digestion and regulates blood sugar.
Legumes (e.g., lentils, chickpeas) – full of protein and fiber.
Leafy greens:

Spinach, kale, collard greens – rich in vitamins, antioxidants, and folate, which support brain health.
Hydration:

Drink plenty of water and green tea – helps with detoxification and hydration.
Healthy fats:

Olive oil, avocados, and nuts – essential for brain health and providing anti-inflammatory effects.
Limit processed sugar and refined carbs:

Avoid sugary drinks, pastries, and refined grains to support overall health and reduce tumor growth risks.

Foods to Avoid:

Processed meats and refined sugars.
High-sodium or high-fat foods.
Alcohol and sugary beverages.







Food Recommendations for Brain Tumor Not Predicted
A balanced and nutrient-dense diet for overall brain health and well-being:

Fruits and Vegetables:

Brightly colored fruits and vegetables (carrots, tomatoes, peppers): Packed with antioxidants.
Bananas and apples for a quick energy boost.
Whole Grains:

Whole-wheat bread, pasta, and cereals for long-lasting energy.
Proteins:

Eggs: Contain choline, which supports brain function.
Lean meats and legumes.
Dairy:

Low-fat milk, yogurt, and cheese for calcium and probiotics.
Healthy Snacks:

Dark chocolate (in moderation): Contains flavonoids for brain health.
Trail mix with dried fruits and nuts.
Healthy Drinks:

Herbal teas and fresh fruit juices without added sugar.
Coconut water for natural electrolytes.
Foods to Avoid:

Deep-fried and junk food.
Excessive caffeine or energy drinks.
General Tips for Both Scenarios:
Portion Control: Avoid overeating to maintain a healthy weight.
Meal Timing: Eat small, frequent meals to stabilize energy levels.
Personalization: Consult with a nutritionist to tailor dietary needs based on the individual’s health and preferences.
