If Heart Disease is Predicted
The focus is on heart-friendly foods that lower cholesterol, control blood pressure, and reduce the risk of further complications:

Whole Grains

Brown rice, quinoa, oatmeal, and whole-grain bread to provide fiber and improve heart health.
Leafy Green Vegetables

Spinach, kale, and broccoli rich in antioxidants and vitamins.
Fatty Fish

Salmon, mackerel, and sardines, which are high in omega-3 fatty acids to reduce inflammation and lower blood pressure.
Nuts and Seeds

Almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, and flaxseeds as sources of healthy fats and protein.
Legumes and Beans

Lentils, chickpeas, and black beans to add protein and fiber while maintaining low cholesterol levels.
Low-Fat Dairy

Yogurt and skim milk to ensure calcium intake without saturated fats.
Fruits

Berries, oranges, and apples, which are high in antioxidants and low in sugar.
Healthy Oils

Olive oil and avocado oil for cooking instead of saturated fats.
Herbs and Spices

Turmeric, garlic, ginger, and cinnamon to enhance flavor and provide health benefits.
Limit or Avoid:

Processed foods, sugary beverages, red meat, fried foods, and excessive salt.


























If Heart Disease Is Not Predicted
While there are fewer restrictions, it's still wise to follow a balanced diet to maintain heart health and overall well-being:

Balanced Carbohydrates

Include whole grains, such as quinoa, bulgur, and barley.
Lean Proteins

Skinless chicken, turkey, eggs, tofu, and fish for muscle building and repair.
Fruits and Vegetables

A colorful variety to ensure a broad spectrum of nutrients.
Healthy Snacks

Trail mix with nuts, seeds, and dried fruit, or whole-grain crackers with hummus.
Dairy

Full-fat or low-fat dairy depending on calorie requirements, such as cheese and milk.
Hydration

Water, herbal teas, and fresh fruit juices with minimal sugar.
Healthy Treats

Dark chocolate (70% cocoa or higher) in moderation.
Occasional Indulgence

Red meat, sweets, and fried foods can be consumed occasionally and in moderation.
Portion Control

Even for healthier foods, avoid overeating to maintain a balanced calorie intake.
Regular Exercise and Lifestyle Habits

While not food-related, this is crucial for both prevention and overall health.
