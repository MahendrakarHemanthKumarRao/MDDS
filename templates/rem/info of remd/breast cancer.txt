Food Recommendations for Breast Cancer Predicted
If a breast cancer diagnosis is predicted, the diet should focus on boosting immunity, reducing inflammation, and supporting overall health. Incorporate foods rich in antioxidants, healthy fats, and cancer-fighting nutrients.

Recommended Foods:
Fruits and Vegetables:

Berries (blueberries, strawberries, raspberries) – high in antioxidants.
Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts) – contain sulforaphane, which may inhibit cancer growth.
Leafy greens (spinach, kale, collards) – rich in vitamins A, C, and E.
Tomatoes – high in lycopene, a powerful antioxidant.
Sweet potatoes – loaded with beta-carotene.
Whole Grains and Fiber-Rich Foods:

Brown rice, quinoa, oats – improve digestion and reduce inflammation.
Legumes (lentils, chickpeas, beans) – high in fiber and plant-based protein.
Healthy Fats:

Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines) – rich in omega-3 fatty acids.
Nuts and seeds (walnuts, chia seeds, flaxseeds) – support heart and brain health.
Olive oil – anti-inflammatory properties.
Proteins:

Organic, lean poultry or plant-based protein.
Tofu or tempeh – soy-based foods that may reduce breast cancer risk.
Herbs and Spices:

Turmeric (with black pepper) – anti-inflammatory.
Garlic – supports immune function.
Ginger – aids digestion and reduces inflammation.
Beverages:

Green tea – contains catechins, powerful antioxidants.
Water – stay hydrated.
Foods to Avoid:
Processed meats (bacon, sausages).
Sugary beverages and snacks.
Excessive alcohol consumption.
High-fat dairy products.
Fried and processed foods.







Food Recommendations for No Breast Cancer Predicted
When breast cancer is not predicted, focus on a balanced diet that promotes general health and prevents future health issues.

Recommended Foods:
Fruits and Vegetables:

Variety of fresh fruits (apples, oranges, bananas, berries).
Seasonal vegetables – ensure a mix of colors for diverse nutrients.
Whole Grains:

Whole wheat bread, barley, farro – provide energy and essential nutrients.
Lean Proteins:

Skinless chicken, turkey, eggs.
Legumes and lentils for vegetarians.
Healthy Fats:

Avocados – support skin and heart health.
Nuts and seeds – excellent snack options.
Dairy:

Low-fat or non-dairy alternatives (almond milk, soy milk).
Hydration:

Water and natural juices without added sugars.
Foods to Enjoy in Moderation:
Red meat (choose lean cuts).
Treats (dark chocolate is better than milk chocolate).
Coffee (stick to 1-2 cups per day).
