Anti-inflammatory Foods

  • Turmeric (contains curcumin) – helps reduce inflammation.
  • Fatty fish (e.g., salmon, mackerel, sardines) – rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which help reduce inflammation and may promote brain health.
  • Berries (e.g., blueberries, strawberries) – rich in antioxidants, which can help protect brain cells from damage.
  • Healthy fats (e.g., olive oil, avocados, nuts) – essential for brain health and providing anti-inflammatory effects.

High-fiber Foods

  • Whole grains (e.g., quinoa, brown rice, oats) – fiber helps in digestion and regulates blood sugar.
  • Legumes (e.g., lentils, chickpeas) – full of protein and fiber.
  • Leafy greens (e.g., spinach, kale, collard greens) – rich in vitamins, antioxidants, and folate, which support brain health.
  • Hydration (e.g., drink plenty of water and green tea) – helps with detoxification and hydration.

Foods to Avoid

  • Processed meats and refined sugars.
  • High-sodium or high-fat foods.
  • Alcohol and sugary beverages.

Additional Tips

  • Small, Frequent Meals: Eating smaller portions more frequently can help manage nausea and energy levels.
  • Fresh, Organic Produce: Prefer organic produce when possible to reduce exposure to pesticides.
  • Consult a Nutritionist/Dietitian: Personalized nutrition advice can help cater to individual health needs and treatment plans.
